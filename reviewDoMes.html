Livro 1:

Nação Dopamina

4 of 5 stars
I wanted to read the book Dopamine Nation by Dr. Anna Lembke to address my own issues with harmful consumption, such as gaming and pornography. With that focus in mind, I liked it because it's extremely accessible, an easy read, and a good introduction to the subject of addiction/compulsive behaviors in today's world. Regardless of any criticism others may have of the book, I personally didn't find anything terribly wrong with it, even if some of the recommendations probably promise too much. Does taking a 10-minute cold shower really improve your cortisol levels for the next four hours? Even so, one of the book's arguments resonated with me: the modern world avoids pain and suffering at all costs. Some people will do anything—ignoring other obvious consequences and others not so obvious—to achieve a state of pleasure, or at least a state free from pain and suffering. We can argue that this has always been true, but that's not a counterargument to the book's ultimate point: moderation is the best path. I'm not defending the steps described by the author as a bible to be followed strictly, but rather as recommendations we can use as a starting point and as a reflection on what might be useful for each of us. One of the author's arguments doesn't work for me, for example. Her claim that stricter religions have more community bonding seems false from my skeptical point of view and my atheistic leaning. In my country, more people seek out liberal religions than extremely strict ones, like Orthodox Judaism or Mormonism. Even so, this "fact" presented by the author—even if it proves true—is useless for an atheist who couldn't benefit from the "spiritual" aspect (if there is any beyond placebo). Likewise, the argument that "family support greatly aids in a addict's recovery" can be false if your family is more of an enemy than truly caring and supportive people. I'd even say that certain families can be triggers for drug use (without shifting the responsibility for the individual's actions onto the toxic family, because that's not always true). In the end, the book helped me reflect on my behaviors that I was already thought was bad for me in the long term. Some of these I'm currently addressing, such as reducing the hours I play games per day, increasing my study hours, cutting down on screen time, reducing the time I spend consuming pornography, exercising, etc. There are still behaviors—even aspects of my personality—that I need to work on, but these seem harder to address in a consistent and structured way, unlike physical exercise. But I can say this book helped me in those aspects, and because of that, I consider it a good book.

Série 1:

The House of the Dragon

For real, Game of Thrones left a huge gap in prime TV. The gap is so wide that even a lessen show like House of the Dragon can seem to be great. Don't get me wrong, i like it a lot. The actors are great, the production seems on par to the original, and I do like many aspects of the story so far. But before touching on what I like, lets state what I did find it to be on the weaker side. Characters here are more likable than on GoT, but not by much like the few likable characters of Knight of the Seven Kingdoms. The problem here is that some characters development are rushed to say the least. Not that I do find it bad to be so many time skips, it works to express what the creators want to express. Its just that Rhaenyra's and Alicent's decisions are somewhat braindead, like, deciding to go to each others cities to talk with the ones (semi or not) responsable for your childrens deaths. Most people are not that "understanding", specially regarding their children. What I like is how they depict the cicle of greed and violence and how that brings ruins to their family and legacy. Even the King Viserys's Ghost stated that the crown ruins and corrupts its wielder.

Série 1:

The Pitt Temporada 2

Te falar que a primeira temporada realmente me impactou. Me fez refletir: se um hospital já é fudido desse jeito nos EUA (que é pra ter uma infraestrutura melhor), imagina como deve ser a vida dos médicos e enfermeiros num hospital público no Brasil.
A segunda temporada aborda mais a situação psicológica do protagonista, o que amei, mostra mais tambem os efeitos de problemas de relacionamento fora e de dentro do ambiente de trabalho afetando o trabalho deles.
É uma daquelas séries em que os protagonistas conseguem ser falhos mas não insuportáveis, tipo How To Get Away with Murder (achei todos eles insuportáveis, Shonda Rhimes já virou escritora de slop a muito tempo).

Mangá 1:

The Ancient Magus' Bride

Gostei da sutileza do relacionamento da protagonista e o Elias. Pensei que seria algo mais controverso, mas do jeito que foi ' desenvolvido, achei mais interessante.

O relacionamento deles é basicamente assexual, mas romantico (pelo menos até o volume 21, vai saber se ele não dá uma de Usagi Drop). A sutileza funciona meio que, para ser honesto, na idea da lolita de 1000 anos. Mas aqui é o oposto, o Elias tem 1000 anos enquanto a Chise é a adolescente, é aqui onde ta a principal diferença obvia.

Parece mais que a autora escreveu, não acho que foi de propósito, uma inversão do que é problematico, em uma fantasia onde o interesse amoroso da protagonista é uma pessoa mais velha, mas ao mesmo tempo possui uma mentalidade incompleta, infantil, "mas ainda mais ainda", o Elias aprende, evolui, amadurece de acordo com a Chise, ele aprende a se comunicar, a Chise aprende a se importar com a vida dela e a se importar com viver.

A idea aqui é que não há, de certo modo, uma lacuna de experiencia, mas ainda eu entenderia se alguem ver problema, pois o Elias ainda tem maior conhecimento, mesmo que ao decorrer da história, parece que a Chise adquire maior poder.
